**Problem Statement 1 — Seasonal Occupancy Analysis**

> _Hotel management needs to understand how booking volumes fluctuate across years, months, and seasons. By analyzing arrival patterns and cancellation rates over time, the database should reveal peak and low demand periods — enabling smarter staffing, pricing, and marketing decisions._

---

**Problem Statement 2 — Guest Profitability Analysis**

> _Not all guests generate equal revenue. This analysis aims to identify which guest segments — defined by customer type, market segment, room type, and length of stay — produce the highest average daily rate (ADR) and lowest cancellation rate, so the hotel can prioritize and target its most profitable customers._

---

**Problem Statement 3 — Guest Loyalty & Repeat Stay Analysis**

> _Retaining guests is more cost-effective than acquiring new ones. This problem investigates what factors — such as meal plan, deposit type, special requests, room assignment accuracy, and previous experience — are associated with guests returning for repeat bookings._

---

**Problem Statement 4 — Corporate Partnership Opportunity**

> _A subset of bookings are linked to company IDs, suggesting organized corporate travel. By identifying which companies send the most guests, their preferred room types, and seasonal patterns, the hotel can evaluate potential partnership agreements to secure stable recurring revenue._
